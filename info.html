Project Information

The Planet XH201 presents an aquatic world inspired by Kepler 138 and Titan, characterized by vast oceans and minimal land. In the deep, sunless waters, life has adapted to rely on chemosynthesis instead of photosynthesis, with unique plant species that thrive at the ocean floor, developing thicker stems to withstand high pressure and more pores for carbon dioxide absorption. Chemosynthesis allows bacteria and other organisms to produce food using chemical energy, forming the foundation of the food web in these dark ecosystems, crucial for supporting life in environments like the Mariana Trench.
